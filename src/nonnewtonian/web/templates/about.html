About this project

Working through a common syllabus with a standard textbook has introductory physics students looking at a progression of physicists all of whom — to within rounding error — look like Newton. That is not quite a historical accident, and not always so benign as the word "accident" implies.

This project started as a class assignment: research someone who has made a significant contribution to physics and who is not a white European man, write a paragraph about them, and figure out where in the textbook their work belongs. The results became a shared, chapter-indexed resource so that any teacher covering, say, energy or nuclear physics can find a relevant scientist and a ready-to-use slide in about a minute.

Why it works

Counter-stereotypical scientist "spotlights" are not just feel-good: giving students repeated, structured exposure to scientists who break the stereotype measurably shifts their sense of who does science (Schinske et al., CBE—Life Sciences Education, 2016).
