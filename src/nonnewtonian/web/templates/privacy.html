Privacy & contact

This is a small teaching project, not a data operation, so there isn't much to say here. I try to collect as little as I can, and you can email me to change or remove anything.

What's stored

  • The writeups, sources, and photos on the public pages, plus a contributor's name when someone chose to give one.
  • Photos are copied onto the server, not hot-linked from somewhere else. If I haven't confirmed a photo's license, it says source unverified and links back to where it came from.
  • If you run a class, I keep the class name, your name, and your email if you give one. I use the email only to send your management link back to you. The server also keeps ordinary web logs, including IP addresses, which I use to cut down on spam.

Student work is public

Intro physics is sometimes taught to students under 18, so one thing to be clear about: an approved entry shows up on a public page. A student is credited by first name and last initial by default, and you can require full anonymity for your class. Whether that works for your students, and whatever consent or media-release rules your school has, is your call, not mine. I'm not trying to collect anything from children under 13.

Fixing or removing something

To fix an error, update a credit, or ask me to take down a name or photo, email me at mglerner@smith.edu. I'll take care of it. Once something has been published under an open license I can't recall the copies other people already made, but I'll remove it from this site and its exports.

Where it lives

It runs on one small server, with encrypted backups kept off-site. I don't run analytics or ads, and nothing on the page loads from a third party.
